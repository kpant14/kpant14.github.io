Pant

I am a Ph.D. candidate in the School of Aeronautics and Astronautics at Purdue University, advised by Dr. Inseok Hwang and Dr. James Goppert. My research focuses on bridging the gap between complex and rigorous mathematical theories and their realization in practice. To this end, I draw ideas from Rigidity Theory, Lie Group Theory, and Optimal Control, and apply them to robots — specifically, uncrewed aerial vehicles (UAVs) — to enhance their robustness, resiliency, and safety against unknown anomalies, disturbances and adversarial cyberattacks.

Previously, I was a Research Scientist at Vikram Sarabhai Space Center (VSSC), ISRO, where I contributed to the development of control systems for Flex Nozzle Control (FNC) and Fin Tip Control (FTC) of the GSLV, GSLV Mk III, PSLV, and SSLV launch vehicles. Before that, I completed my B.Tech. in Electronics Engineering from IIT (BHU) Varanasi.

Most recently, I have been working on dynamics modeling, planning, and control of tethered-kite systems in collaboration with the Toyota Research Institute of North America (TRINA) on the Mothership Project. My focus is specifically on designing resilient planning and control frameworks for kites, with applications to Airborne Wind Energy (AWE) harvesting and Atmospheric Sensing.
